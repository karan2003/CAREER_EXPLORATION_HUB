COURSE DETAILS: INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

WHAT DOES AN IT CAREER ENTAIL?

Are you interested in pursuing a career in information technology (IT)? IT is a fast-growing and dynamic field that offers a variety of opportunities and challenges for people who are passionate about technology and innovation. IT professionals use computers, storage, and networking devices to create, process, store, share, and secure electronic data. They work for IT departments and consulting firms across all industries, providing IT services and solutions to meet the needs and goals of their clients and organizations.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for IT careers was $81,430 in May 2015, considerably higher than that for all occupations. The demand for IT workers is also expected to grow faster than the average for all occupations, as more businesses and organizations rely on technology to operate and compete in the global market.

If you want to enter IT domain and enjoy the benefits of a rewarding and lucrative career, you will need to have the right education, skills, and experience. In this guide, we will show you how to get started in the field of IT, from the various types of careers available to the degrees necessary to secure employment.

Types of IT Careers

IT is a broad and diverse field that encompasses many different areas and specialties. Some of the most common and popular IT careers include:

  • Software developers: They design, develop, test, and maintain software applications and systems that run on computers, mobile devices, or the web. They use programming languages, frameworks, and tools to create software products and solutions that meet the specifications and requirements of their clients and users.
  • Web developers: They create and maintain websites and web applications that run on the internet or intranets. They use web technologies, such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, Ruby, etc. to design, code, and implement web pages and features that are user-friendly, functional, and attractive.
  • Database administrators: They manage and maintain databases that store and organize data for various purposes and applications. They use database management systems, such as SQL, Oracle, MySQL, etc. to create, modify, backup, restore, and secure databases and ensure their optimal performance and availability.
  • Network administrators: They plan, install, configure, and maintain networks that connect computers and devices within an organization or across the internet. They use network devices, such as routers, switches, firewalls, etc. to ensure the network's functionality, security, and efficiency.
  • Information security analysts: They protect the information and systems of an organization or a client from unauthorized access, use, modification, or destruction. They use security tools, such as antivirus, encryption, firewall, etc. to monitor, detect, prevent, and respond to cyberattacks and threats.
  • Computer support specialists: They provide technical assistance and support to computer users who encounter problems or issues with their hardware, software, or network. They use troubleshooting techniques, diagnostic tools, and communication skills to identify, analyze, and resolve computer-related problems and queries.

These are just some of the examples of IT careers that you can pursue. There are many other IT roles and specialties that you can explore and choose from, depending on your interests, skills, and goals.

IT Degree Programs

To enter IT domain, you will need to have a relevant and recognized degree in IT or a related field. IT degree programs vary widely depending on the institution issuing the credential. From traditional colleges and universities to accredited online and distance learning degree programs, educational opportunities are available to suit nearly any budget or schedule.

Some schools offer certificate courses in specialized areas of IT, such as web development, database administration, network security, etc. These courses are usually short-term and focused, and they can help you gain specific skills and knowledge in a particular IT domain. However, they may not be sufficient or recognized for some IT careers or positions that require a higher level of education or qualification.

Other schools offer associate, bachelor's, or master's degree programs in IT or a related field, such as computer science, software engineering, information systems, etc. These programs are designed to produce well-rounded students with broad-based knowledge and skills in many different areas of IT. They usually cover topics such as programming, data structures, algorithms, databases, networks, operating systems, software engineering, web development, information security, etc. They also include general education courses, such as mathematics, physics, English, etc. to enhance the students' academic and professional competencies.

Depending on the level and duration of the program, an IT degree can take anywhere from two to six years to complete. The admission requirements and tuition fees also vary depending on the school and the program. Generally, you will need to have a high school diploma or equivalent, and meet the minimum GPA and standardized test scores required by the school. You may also need to submit letters of recommendation, personal statements, or portfolios to demonstrate your interest and aptitude for IT.

Online and Hybrid IT Degree Programs

If you are looking for IT degree programs, you'll find both on-campus and distance-based choices. An online program could be a good choice if you want to enroll in a school that is far away, but not move from where you live. Online IT degree programs come in many different shapes and sizes. These include:

  • Hybrid programs: These are a blend of campus-based and distance learning courses. You will need to attend some classes on campus, while completing others online. This can give you the best of both worlds, as you can benefit from the flexibility and convenience of online learning, as well as the interaction and support of face-to-face learning.
  • Fully online programs: These are programs that are delivered entirely online, without any campus visits or requirements. You will access all the course materials, assignments, lectures, discussions, and exams through an online platform or learning management system. You will also communicate and collaborate with your instructors and classmates through email, chat, video conferencing, etc. This can give you the most freedom and flexibility, as you can study at your own pace and place, without any geographical or time constraints.
  • Accelerated online programs: These are programs that are designed to help you complete your degree faster than the normal duration. You will take more courses per term, or take courses that are shorter and more intensive than the regular ones. This can help you save time and money, as well as advance your career sooner. However, you will also need to have more dedication and discipline, as the workload and pace will be more demanding and challenging.
  • Joint degree programs: These are programs that allow you to earn two degrees at the same time, or in a shorter time than earning them separately. For example, you can earn a bachelor's and a master's degree in IT in five years, instead of six. Or, you can earn a degree in IT and another field, such as business, engineering, or education. This can help you broaden your knowledge and skills, as well as increase your career options and opportunities.

Most online IT degree programs also use an interactive learning management system that allows you to submit assignments, ask questions, and have discussions with other students. You will also have access to various online resources and services, such as libraries, tutoring, counseling, etc. to support your learning and success.

How to Choose an IT Degree Program

Choosing an IT degree program can be a daunting task, as there are many factors and options to consider. Here are some tips to help you make an informed and wise decision:

  • Identify your goals and interests: Before you start looking for IT degree programs, you should have a clear idea of what you want to achieve and what you are passionate about. What kind of IT career do you want to pursue? What kind of IT skills and knowledge do you want to acquire? What kind of IT projects or problems do you want to work on? These questions can help you narrow down your choices and find the programs that match your goals and interests.
  • Research the programs and schools: Once you have a list of potential IT degree programs, you should do some research and compare them based on various criteria, such as curriculum, accreditation, reputation, faculty, cost, duration, admission requirements, graduation outcomes, etc. You can use online tools, such as [College Navigator](https://www.markdownguide.org/basic-syntax/), [U.S. News & World Report](https://zapier.com/blog/markdown-html-export/), [Niche](https://zapier.com/blog/beginner-ultimate-guide-markdown/), etc. to find and evaluate IT degree programs and schools.
  • Consider your preferences and circumstances: You should also consider your personal preferences and circumstances when choosing an IT degree program. For example, do you prefer to study on campus or online? Do you have the time and money to complete a full-time or part-time program? Do you have any prior work or academic experience in IT? Do you have any special needs or accommodations? These factors can affect your decision and satisfaction with your chosen program.

COURSE DETAILS: LAND SURVEY

WHAT DOES AN IT CAREER ENTAIL?

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree The Geospatial Sciences (Building Information Modelling) MSc teaches students the theory, tools and techniques for working with geospatial data, from acquisition to processing to analysis. Students have the opportunity to engage and work with academics and industrial partners on cutting-edge research through seminars and a dissertation. The specialist Building Information Modelling (BIM) programme will educate students in the geometric and semantic aspects of BIM and the integrated management of geospatial and BIM-related data. This includes principles of surveying, 3D reality capture, a general overview of technologies related to mapping sciences, geo-information science and 3D geometric modelling.
Careers
Students graduating with a degree in Geospatial Sciences (Building Information Modelling) will be equipped for a diverse range of roles, including BIM or surveying manager, geospatial data scientist, geospatial software developer and consultant. Graduates go on to work for a variety of organisations including major infrastructure projects, engineering consultancies, geospatial software companies and national mapping agencies. Our students establish strong links with industry through the industrial seminar series and industrial research projects. Additionally, many graduates from our programmes go on to PhD research at UCL or elsewhere.
Employability
Students will gain a range of technical skills in geospatial science that are highly valued by employers, including the use of specialist software for surveying, Reality Capture, BIM and GIS (e.g. Cyclone, PhotoScan, Revit, QGIS, FME); programming, web and software development (e.g. Python, R, MATLAB); geospatial data processing and management; geospatial data visualisation and analysis.

Career Objectives

The areas in which the knowledge and technology of Surveying are today applied include:

  • Precise determination of the figure of the earth and of other planets in the solar system
  • Measurement and mapping of topography
  • Measurement and determination of orbits of artificial satellites
  • Measurement and determination of the dynamics of the earths crust
  • Determination and analysis of the earths gravity field
  • Precise positioning on, or near, the earths surface using both terrestrial and satellite technology techniques
  • Navigation and guidance of craft using satellite and inertial navigation systems technology
  • Vehicle tracking and intelligent transport systems (ITS)

COURSE DETAILS: MECHATRONICS ENGINEERING

WHAT IS IT ABOUT ENGINEERING?

Engineering is the application of science and maths to solve problems. Engineers figure out how things work and use for scientific discoveries. Scientists and inventors often get credit for innovations that advance human condition, but it is the engineers who are instrumental in making these innovatons available to the global population. Engineering is one of the corner stones of STEM(science, technology, engineering, mathematics) education, an interdisciplinary curriculum designed to motivate students to learn about the evolving world. A career in engineering requires continual professional development to remain relevant and effective in today's rapidly changing global society. There are currently more than 15 million engineers across various disciplines worldwide and with the surge in technological advancements and preparedness, anyone can be a part of it.

Engineering spreads over multiple fields such as bio-engineering to the more common mechanical engineering courses. Engineering is a hands-on course that relies more on practicality and not theory based studies. However, engineering optons such as software engineering offer the rare pleasure of being a mind based field where traditional college institutions as well as highly-acclaimed online programmes strive to provide high grade content and knowledge. All is not lost though, since engineering favours both the active and passive. An engineering degree takes upto tho 5 years to acquire whereas the masters and PhDs take 2 years each.

To successfully design and engineer solutions for today's and tomorrow's rapidly changing and expanding global contexts, in which people are confronted with new opportunities and challenges each day, engineering programs should be training their students to become broad based professionals, who are aware of the actual needs, values and behaviors of the people that use their solutions in their everyday life, work or play. This paper argues that in order to built such an awareness, engineering students should acquire direct, first-hand experiences of real people in real contexts. It presents a number of techniques that can be used to gain such experiences.

Engineering opens a learner up to greater heights with the right skills and mindset. It is important to point out that engieering experiences an influx of aspiring students each and every year making it a highly competitive field where the resilient and committed survive to emerge on top. However, don not forget to have fun while you are at it. The field enables you to interact with new technology and people with various banks of knowledge to offer in your journey from an amateur aspirant to a fully-fledged engineer and put you well above most in the job market, let alone equip you with skills to solve some of the world's problems.
